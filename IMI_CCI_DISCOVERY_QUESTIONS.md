# IMI CCI-Style Management Discovery Questions

Use these questions for a severe-service control valve management conversation. Keep the tone consultative and operational. Do not imply partnership with IMI CCI or use protected branding.

---

## VP Business Development

1. How many severe-service valve proposals does the team handle per month or quarter?
2. What percentage of proposals are high-value, strategic, or technically complex bids?
3. What is the average bid value range for severe-service opportunities?
4. How do you currently track win rate by proposal type, customer, application, or industry?
5. Where do you see the biggest commercial risk today: late response, incomplete compliance, technical exceptions, pricing delay, or approval bottlenecks?
6. How early does management get visibility into whether a bid is technically and commercially ready?
7. Which proposal KPIs would matter most to leadership: turnaround time, throughput, win rate, engineering hours, compliance score, or margin protection?
8. If proposal cycle time could be reduced by 20-30%, where would that create the most value?

---

## Proposal Director

1. What is the typical turnaround time from RFP receipt to first proposal draft?
2. Which parts of the proposal process consume the most manual effort?
3. How often do proposal teams reuse historical proposal sections, and how is that reuse controlled?
4. Where do proposal teams lose time: RFP reading, compliance matrix preparation, engineering inputs, document formatting, or approvals?
5. How do you assign owners for technical, commercial, compliance, and document sections?
6. How many review cycles are typical before final submission?
7. What causes late-stage proposal rework most often?
8. How do you know whether a proposal is complete enough for management review?
9. What would a “good pilot result” look like for the proposal organization?

---

## Engineering Manager

1. How many proposal requests require application engineering support each week or month?
2. Which proposal questions repeatedly require senior engineering input?
3. Where does engineering spend time re-explaining similar severe-service considerations?
4. How do engineers currently document assumptions, risks, deviations, and clarifications?
5. What is the average engineering effort per complex severe-service bid?
6. Which topics create the biggest review burden: cavitation, flashing, noise, erosion, materials, actuation, leakage class, testing, or standards?
7. How are internal sizing tools connected to proposal documentation today?
8. What type of AI assistance would be acceptable if engineering remains the final reviewer?
9. Which proposal-stage outputs would help engineering most: requirement summary, risk flags, clarification questions, reusable notes, or compliance mapping?

---

## Application Engineering

1. What information is usually missing or unclear when an RFP first reaches application engineering?
2. Which severe-service conditions are hardest to identify quickly from customer documents?
3. How often do you need to ask customers for clarification before preparing a reliable proposal response?
4. What historical content do you wish was easier to find and reuse?
5. How do you currently prepare technical notes for cavitation, flashing, noise, materials, or special trims?
6. How do you track assumptions and exceptions so they are reflected in the final proposal?
7. Which technical bid evaluation items take the longest to complete?
8. What would make proposal-stage engineering review faster without reducing technical control?
9. Where should AI be restricted or clearly marked as requiring engineering validation?

---

## Compliance Lead

1. How is the compliance matrix prepared today, and who owns final coverage?
2. How many RFP requirements typically need explicit compliance or exception responses?
3. What percentage of compliance effort is spent locating requirements versus writing responses?
4. How do you track TBE completion status and open items?
5. Which compliance gaps are most commonly discovered late in the proposal process?
6. How are customer standards, documentation requirements, testing requirements, and deviations reviewed?
7. What evidence does management need before approving submission?
8. How do you handle version control across spreadsheets, proposal documents, and emails?
9. What compliance metrics would be useful in a 30-day pilot: coverage percentage, open exceptions, response time, owner aging, or approval cycle?
