## Slide 2: Why Severe-Service Valve Proposals Are Difficult

- Severe-service bids involve high-pressure drop, cavitation, flashing, noise, erosion, vibration, and material selection risks.
- Requirements are spread across RFPs, datasheets, specifications, standards, deviations, and customer formats.
- Proposal quality depends heavily on senior application engineering experience.
- Small interpretation errors can create technical, commercial, or compliance exposure.
- Customer evaluation often includes detailed technical bid evaluation requirements.

Suggested visual: Complexity map showing RFP inputs, process conditions, valve selection, compliance matrix, drawings, and approvals.

Speaker notes:  
“These proposals are difficult because they are not just commercial responses. They are engineering judgments packaged into a customer-facing commitment. The challenge is coordinating technical interpretation, compliance, drawings, historical knowledge, and approvals under deadline pressure.”

---

## Slide 3: Current Proposal Workflow Pain Points

- RFP review is manual and depends on individual reading depth and availability.
- Senior engineers are repeatedly asked to answer similar technical questions.
- Historical proposals are reused inconsistently, often through memory or folder search.
- Compliance and TBE tracking is fragmented across spreadsheets, emails, and documents.
- Management visibility is often delayed until late-stage review.

Suggested visual: “Current state” workflow with handoffs between sales, proposals, engineering, compliance, and management.

Speaker notes:  
“Most proposal teams have good people and useful templates. The issue is that knowledge is trapped in documents, inboxes, and individual experts. WinsProposal is intended to bring that intelligence into one repeatable workflow.”

## Slide 4: WinsProposal Solution Overview

- Upload RFP packages and extract technical, commercial, and compliance requirements into a structured workspace.
- Reuse approved historical content through a Knowledge Vault aligned to valve subtype and application context.
- Generate proposal-stage engineering intelligence, risks, assumptions, deviations, and clarifications.
- Track TBE and compliance coverage before final submission.
- Export management-ready proposal documents with supporting visuals and review evidence.

Suggested visual: Platform overview: RFP upload, extraction, Knowledge Vault, engineering intelligence, compliance/TBE, export, dashboard.

Speaker notes:  
“WinsProposal acts as a proposal intelligence layer. It does not replace sizing tools, ERP, CRM, or engineering approval. It organizes the bid response workflow so teams can make better decisions earlier and move faster with traceability.”

## Slide 5: Knowledge Vault: Preserving Senior Engineering Knowledge

- Captures approved proposal language, technical notes, deviations, clarifications, and lessons learned.
- Organizes knowledge by valve type, severe-service condition, industry, customer, standard, and application.
- Reduces repeated dependence on senior engineers for recurring proposal content.
- Helps new proposal engineers access proven language with context and review discipline.
- Creates a living institutional memory across bids and regions.

Suggested visual: Knowledge Vault library with tags such as cavitation, flashing, noise, trim selection, metallurgy, actuator, and standards.

Speaker notes:

- Helps proposal teams prepare stronger technical bid evaluation responses.
- Supports review evidence for management, engineering, and compliance sign-off.
- Reduces late-stage surprises before submission.

## Slide 7: Proposal-Stage Engineering Intelligence

- Summarizes key process conditions, service risks, and proposal assumptions from RFP documents.
- Highlights severe-service considerations such as cavitation, flashing, noise, erosion, materials, leakage class, and actuation.
- Produces engineering clarification questions for customer or internal review.
- Identifies proposal risks before detailed design and final commercial commitment.
- Complements internal sizing and selection tools by improving proposal interpretation and documentation.

Suggested visual: Engineering intelligence panel showing extracted conditions, risk flags, assumptions, and clarification questions.

Speaker notes:  
“This is proposal-stage intelligence. It is not trying to become the final authority on valve sizing. It helps the team identify what matters, what is unclear, and what needs engineering attention before the bid moves too far.”
